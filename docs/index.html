Crowsetta is an open-source Android application and research project that classifies North American crow vocalizations into behaviorally meaningful categories — with calibrated confidence, scientific citations, and a deliberate refusal to pretend to know what crows are saying.

§ 01

The premise, stated honestly

Crow vocalizations are real, structured, and rich with information. They are also not a human language and not yet decoded into one. This project respects both facts.

What Crowsetta does

  • Detects whether an audio clip contains a North American crow
  • Distinguishes American Crow from Fish Crow within their overlapping range
  • Classifies the call into one of 7 well-attested categories with calibrated confidence
  • Surfaces a plain-English behavioral interpretation grounded in peer-reviewed literature
  • Provides a deeper-dive layer with the spectrogram, all class probabilities, and citations
  • Lets the user record sensor-rich behavioral context when a call is unfamiliar — feeding a research-quality observation log

What Crowsetta deliberately does not do

  • Translate crow calls into English sentences
  • Claim that "three caws means danger" or anything of that genre
  • Confidently identify individual crows without controlled enrollment data
  • Decode multi-call sequences as syntactic structure
  • Pretend that pop-science folk-beliefs about crow language are scientifically supported
  • Ship results that are unverifiable, uncalibrated, or unsourced

"Caws encode information on caller sex, identity, and behavioural context — but the same acoustic call type can carry different meanings in different contexts. A clean call→meaning dictionary does not yet exist for the American Crow."

after Mates, Tarter, Ha, Clark & McGowan (2015), Bioacoustics 24:63–80; and Pendergraft & Marzluff (2019), Animal Behaviour 150:39–57
§ 02

The seven calls we classify

A working taxonomy distilled from the Birds of the World American Crow account (Verbeek et al. 2024), the Fish Crow account (McGowan 2020), and the Pendergraft / Marzluff / Clark research programs. Per-class accuracy varies; common categories are stronger.

01

Territorial Caw

"Long-distance caw" · bouts of 2–5 (range 1–9)

The familiar long-distance signal — typically delivered from a perch, repeated in bouts. Functions as territorial advertisement and family-group cohesion. Audible across a roughly 0.5–1.0 km territory.

Brown 1985 · Parr 1997 · Thompson 1968

02

Alarm / Mobbing Caw

"Inflected alarm caw" · rapid · persistent

Structurally distinct from the territorial caw. Given when a perceived threat — hawk, owl, cat, unfamiliar person — is detected, often persisting for many minutes and recruiting other crows into a mob.

Birds of the World 2024 · Mates et al. 2015

03

Assembly Call

Recruitment to mob or roost · clustered bouts

The recruitment vocalization Richards & Thompson (1978) identified by acoustic manipulation experiments — calls that bring conspecifics in. Closely related functionally to alarm calling but distinguishable in structure.

Richards & Thompson 1978 · Behaviour 64:184–203

04

Scold

Harsh · sustained · directed

Often confused with the alarm caw but typically directed at a specific target (a perched raptor, a predator on the ground, a person near a nest). Tends to persist in shorter, more focused bursts than diffuse alarm calling.

Birds of the World 2024 · McGowan corr.

05

Beg / Juvenile

Nestling churrs · fledgling chee-aps · wa-eeks

A developmental sequence of squeaks, gargling sounds, and "chee-ap" calls from nestling through fledgling stages. Acoustically similar to adult Fish Crow calls — a known false-positive risk for any classifier.

Kilham 1989 · Birds of the World 2024

06

Rattle / Knock

Female-associated · courtship & cohesion

A distinctive non-caw vocalization — a rapid, mechanical sound roughly described as a wooden rattle. Most often given by females; associated with pair-bonding and family-group contexts. Highly diagnostic when present.

Birds of the World 2024 · Marzluff field obs.

07

Food / Short Call

Brief · context-specific · loosely interpretable

Pendergraft & Marzluff (2019) showed crows give measurably shorter calls in the presence of food than in its absence. The call carries information that listeners do not respond to in any simple way — a reminder that crow signaling is multidimensional, not lexical.

Pendergraft & Marzluff 2019 · Pendergraft et al. 2021

+

Other / Subsong

A repertoire of 20+ acoustically distinct types remains

American Crows have more than twenty distinct call types in addition to the seven above, including a quiet "subsong" of mixed coos, caws, and grating notes given at rest. Most are too rare in public corpora to classify reliably. Crowsetta routes these through the unknown-call observation flow.

McGowan · Bird Academy · Verbeek et al. 2024

§ 03

How it works

A pretrained bioacoustic embedding model carries the heavy lifting; a small classifier head, trained on hand-labeled crow audio, maps embeddings to call types. All inference runs on-device.

A

Audio capture

AudioRecord · UNPROCESSED source · 48 kHz · 16-bit PCM · 3-second window

→
B

Embedding

Perch 2.0 or BirdNET 2.4, frozen, ~12M params, exported to TFLite int8

→
C

Classifier head

2-layer MLP · 7 call-type classes · trained on ~1k hand-labeled segments

→
D

Calibration & UI

Temperature-scaled probabilities · 5-step confidence display · deeper-dive citations · unknown-call flow

Why pretrained embeddings. Ghani, Denton, Kahl & Klinck (2023) showed that global birdsong embeddings transfer to fine-grained call-type and dialect classification with remarkably small task-specific training sets. We exploit that result. The first model trained from scratch on 1,000 crow segments would learn species ID and not much else; the same 1,000 segments fine-tune a small head on a frozen Perch trunk into a usable call-type classifier.

§ 04

Roadmap

A 12–14 week plan with explicit MVP / stretch boundaries.

  1. 01

    Data assembly & baseline classifier weeks 1–3

    Pull amecro / fiscro audio from Xeno-canto v3 and BirdCLEF 2022/2023. Hand-label 500–1,000 segments into the 7-class taxonomy. Train baseline (BirdNET-Analyzer custom head + Perch 2.0 + logistic regression). Pick the winner.

  2. 02

    Model export week 4

    TFLite int8 quantization. Held-out validation including a geographic holdout (East-coast train, West-coast test).

  3. 03

    Android skeleton weeks 5–7

    Kotlin · Jetpack Compose · TFLite Interpreter. Working "press button → see top-3 with calibrated confidence" loop.

  4. 04

    Dual-layer UI weeks 8–9

    Consumer surface (one primary card, plain-English interpretation) with a "Deeper Dive" pane (spectrogram, top-5 bars, citations linked to the canonical papers).

  5. 05

    Behavioral observation capture weeks 10–11

    CameraX, FusedLocationProvider, SensorManager, Open-Meteo. Versioned JSON schema. Local-only by default; opt-in cloud sync stub.

  6. 06

    Calibration & demo prep week 12

    Confidence threshold tuning on held-out validation. Project report. Demo recordings for live presentation.

  7. 07

    Stretch — sequence parsing & individual ID post-MVP

    BiGRU over per-caw embeddings for bout-level labels. Contrastive head for "matches your local roost" individual-ID hint. Both research-grade; honest about limitations.

§ 06

What this cannot do

Stated explicitly so it is harder for anyone, including us, to forget.

  • i.No public model maps crow calls to human-language meanings. Our outputs are probable behavioral categories, not translations.
  • ii.The same acoustic call type can carry different meanings in different contexts. Mates et al. and Pendergraft & Marzluff established this clearly.
  • iii.Public crow corpora are species-labeled, not call-type-labeled. Performance is bounded by manual labeling effort.
  • iv.Geographic generalization is uneven. Pacific-coast and continental American Crow vocalizations differ; juvenile begging resembles adult Fish Crow.
  • v.Different Android devices have different microphone DSP defaults. The UNPROCESSED audio source mitigates but does not eliminate this.
  • vi.User-supplied behavioral labels in the unknown-call flow are candidate labels. They are not ground truth.
